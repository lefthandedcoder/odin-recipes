Winter Warmer Oatmeal Raisin Cookies

by Christian Dye, published December 27, 2021

As winter howls down from the mountains and besets the land in a frigid embrace, nothing warms the hearth more than a welcoming plate of oatmeal cookies. Best served with fresh frothy milk, these cookies are a must for any winter occasion. Light the fire, pass around the plate, and gather round for tales of days gone by.

Ingredients

Yield: 26-30 cookies

  • 1 c softened unsalted butter
  • 1/2 c packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 Tbsp vanilla extract
  • 1 Tbsp molasses
  • 1 1/2 c flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 3 c rolled oats
  • 1 c raisins
  • 1/2 c chopped toasted walnuts

Steps

  1. Cream softened butter and sugars until smooth. Add eggs and mix until combined. Scrape sides and bottom as needed. Add vanilla and molasses and mix until combined.
  2. In a seperate bowl, whisk flour, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt together. Add to wet ingredients and mix until combined. Add in oats, raisins, and walnuts. Chill for 30-60 minutes (Optional but helps cookies keep their form better).
  3. Preheat oven to 350Â°F. Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  4. Roll balls of dough and place on baking sheets at spaced intervals. Bake for 12-14 minutes or until lightly browned on sides. Remove from oven and cool.
