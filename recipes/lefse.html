Lefse

by Christian Dye, published December 27, 2021

Traditionally made with potatoes, flour, butter, and cream, this soft flatbread best served with sweet butter or whipped cream. Alternatively, lefse can be eaten with savory, salty foods. Throw out your dinner rolls and serve a platter of hot lefse for dinner tonight.

Ingredients

Yield: 16-20 lefse

  • 2 lbs. potatoes (approx. 5 large potatoes)
  • 1/4 c heavy cream
  • 1 Tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 c flour

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 175Â°F.
  2. Peel potatoes and coarsely chop into 1-inch chunks.
  3. Boil a pot of water and add potatoes. Cook until fork-tender, about 15-20 minutes.
  4. Drain potatoes and arrange on parchment-lined baking sheet. Place in oven for 15 minutes to dry further.
  5. Remove potatoes from oven and pass through ricer.
  6. Mix in heavy cream, butter, and salt.
  7. Mix well and place dough in fridge, covered, overnight.
  8. Preheat skillet. Cut flour into chilled dough and knead until smooth.
  9. Divide dough into 16-20 biscuit-sized balls.
  10. Flour board and rolling pin. ROll out each ball into a 12-inch circle, dusting with flour as needed.
  11. Once skillet is very hot, lift lefse circle with handle of a flat wooden spatula. Transfer to skillet.
  12. Cook until brown spots begin to appear.
  13. Flip and cook on the other side.
  14. Remove to a plate lined with a damp clean cloth to cool. Cover with another damp cloth. Repeat until all dough has been cooked.
  15. Serve lefse with fresh butter or whipped heavy cream.
  16. Alternatively, lefse can be served with cream cheese and deli meats or eggs and bacon.
