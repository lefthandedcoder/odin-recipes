Odin's Lutefisk

Lutefisk with Red Potatoes and Mashed Peas

by Christian Dye, published December 27, 2021

Lutefisk, a staple of those now passed on to Valhalla found upon any decent julebord buffet, is a hearty dish of dried whitefish that has been pickled in lye. Gelatinous in texture, this dish has helped the people of Odin survive many a harsh and bitter winter. After it has been soaked in cold water for roughly 2 weeks, this fish is best served with potatoes, mashed peas, white sauce, mustard, and bacon. And of course, no meal tastes quite as good without a heavy mug of mead and the laughter of a good friend. Drink up and dig in!

Ingredients

Serves: 4-6

Lutefisk

  • 1 piece of dried lutefisk, cut into 6 inch lengths
  • 2 Tbsp lye

Potatoes

  • 1/4 c oil
  • 2 Tbsp butter
  • 5 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 2 1/4 lbs. red potatoes
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh dill sprigs, to taste

Mashed Peas

  • 10 oz. frozen peas
  • 1/4 c heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Steps

Lutefisk

  1. Soak fish in clear water for 3 days.
  2. Add lye into 1 gallon of water.
  3. Soak fish for 3 days in solution.
  4. Soak for 4 days in clear water, changing water once a day.
  5. Tie fish loosely in a square of cheese cloth.
  6. Drop in enamel pot of boiling water.
  7. Cook for 10 minutes until well done.
  8. Remove from cheese cloth, place on platter, and debone.

Potatoes

  1. Preheat oven to 400Â°F.
  2. In a large bowl, beat together oil, butter, garlic, and dill until combined.
  3. Scrub potatoes clean and cut into 1-inch chunks.
  4. Toss potatoes in bowl. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Arrange potatoes in a single layer on a foil-lined baking sheet. Roast, turning once or twice halfway through, until golden brown, 45 to 60 minutes.
  6. Remove from oven, season again with salt and pepper to taste and garnish with dill.

Mashed Peas

  1. Bring a pot of lightly salted water to boil. Add frozen peas, and cook for 3 minutes, until tender.
  2. Drain peas and pour into large blender. Add cream, butter, salt and pepper, and process until blended. Season to taste and serve.
